# The Declaration of AIndependence

*A coding constitution you can enforce no matter who — or what — is holding the keyboard.*

---

A coding agent is capable, tireless, and confident — and confidence is the hazard. Left
to its own account of itself, it will report *done* before it is done, assume what it was
never told, and forget between sessions what it just learned. A document full of good
intentions does not change that. **A rule you cannot enforce is a rule you do not have.**

So this regime governs **the work, not the worker** — and it declares independence on
three axes:

- **Independence of vendor.** No lock to Anthropic, Google, OpenAI, Cursor, or whoever
  ships next. The law is plain Python a git hook runs — un-bypassable wherever the work
  meets a shared remote and CI — advisory and human-backed where none does — and beholden to no single tool's blessing anywhere. Per-vendor
  in-loop hooks are conveniences layered on top, never the law.
- **Independence of model.** The gate judges the *verdict*, not its author. The cheapest
  local model, the most capable frontier one, and a person at a terminal are weighed by
  the same schema. Routing advises; it does not own the truth.
- **Independence of coder — human or machine.** The law is written so a human engineer
  can read it and run it by hand, with no special tooling to understand it. Whoever holds
  the keyboard is a subject of the same constitution, and a beneficiary of the same proof.

We hold three things to be load-bearing:

1. **Enforce, don't document.** Every rule descends to the cheapest medium that can make
   it *true* — a hook, a permission, a test. Prose is the medium of last resort.
2. **Assume an independent audit — and make it real.** Self-certification is not evidence.
   Work is *done* only when a reviewer that could not see the author's intent has rendered
   a verdict the pipeline refuses to skip. The auditor is built so it *cannot* rationalize
   a pass — not merely forbidden from it.
3. **The human stays legible and sovereign.** The constitution is grep-able prose; the
   gate accepts a verdict a person can write; the irreversible act is always the human's
   to perform. Independence from the machine includes independence *of the human within it.*

This is not independence *from* AI. It is independence *with* it — the terms on which a
person and a model can build together without either having to take the other's word for it.
